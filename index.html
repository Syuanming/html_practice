Charlie Brown

The main character of the comic strip Peanuts, Charles "Charlie" Brown, is syndicated in daily and Sunday newspapers all over the world. Charlie Brown, who is portrayed as a "lovable loser," is a beloved and well-known cartoon character and one of the great American archetypes.

History

  • 1940s-1950s: First Peanuts strip, October 2, 1950.
  • 1960s: the Peanuts comic strip entered what most readers consider to be its Golden Age, and Charlie Brown.
  • 1970s: For this decade, the character appeared on twelve Peanuts television specials that were produced as a result of the success of the prior ones.
  • 1980s: Charlie Brown went on to feature in fourteen more television specials, two of which are musicals.
  • 1990s: Six television specials featuring Charlie Brown were produced during this decade.

Personality

  1. Charlie Brown is a shy, meek, kind, innocent, gentle-hearted character with many anxieties.
  2. Charlie Brown cares very deeply for his family and friends, even if he was maltreated by them.
  3. Christopher Caldwell has stated that "What makes Charlie Brown such a rich character is that he's not purely a loser.

Other information

In the strip from November 3, 1950, Charlie Brown said that he was "only four years old". However, he aged over the next two decades, being six years old as of November 17, 1957, and "eight-and-a-half years old" by July 11, 1979. Later references continue to peg Charlie Brown as being approximately eight years old.

The character's creator, Charles M. Schulz, said that Charlie Brown "has to be the one who suffers, because he is a caricature of the average person.

Charlie Brown is characterized as a person who frequently suffers, and as a result, is usually nervous and lacks self-confidence.
